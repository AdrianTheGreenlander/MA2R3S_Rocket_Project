Overview

A rocket is a propulsive device that uses principles of action and reaction to
generate thrust and motion. It burns a fueloxidizer mixture in a combustion
chamber, creating high-pressure, high-velocity exhaust gases that are expelled
through a nozzle, imparting momentum to the rocket and propelling it forward.
